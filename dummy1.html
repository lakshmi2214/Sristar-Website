The Spectrum of Gold Colors: Insights from the Expert

Gold's allure lies not just in its economic value but in its cultural significance across civilizations. Discussing the types of gold, Srikanth reveals, "We have majorly three types of gold in the industry: yellow gold, rose gold, and white gold.

Yellow Gold

"Yellow gold is the purest type of gold, made from pure gold and alloyed metals like zinc, copper, and silver," Srikanth explains. Its versatility and hypoallergenic properties make it a jewel in the crown of the gold industry.

The Crown Jewel: Yellow Gold’s Eminence

When asked about the most valuable gold type, Srikanth’s expertise shines through: “Yellow gold. It has a purity of 91.6 percent.” This declaration of yellow gold’s dominance highlights its lasting attractiveness and value in the market.

The Art and Science of Purity Testing:

In the quest for authenticity, the gold industry has seen significant advancements in testing methods. Srikanth outlines the evolution from traditional to modern techniques: “The organized sectors will use state-of-the-art technology. It is a non-destructive method. In older days, it was about rubbing the gold, but now, with modern technology, gold is kept inside a machine where the rays will be passed through the metal, and in 30 seconds, the exact results will be given.”

He also touches on traditional methods that persist due to their simplicity and directness, such as the “rubbing on a touchstone” and the use of “gold testing pens,” though he acknowledges their shortcomings when compared to modern technologies.
